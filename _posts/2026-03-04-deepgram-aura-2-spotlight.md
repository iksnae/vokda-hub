Deepgram is best known for speech-to-text, but their TTS offering — **Aura-2** — deserves attention on its own terms. It's doing something most cloud TTS providers haven't prioritized: making voices sound competent in specialized domains.

## The Voice Catalog

Aura-2 ships with **40+ English voices**, named after mythological figures: Thalia, Odysseus, Harmonia, Theia, Electra, Arcas, Amalthea, Helena, Hyperion, Apollo, Luna, and more. The naming scheme is a deliberate choice — memorable, distinct, and avoids the generic "Voice 1" problem that plagues catalog browsing.

Localization covers **US, Australian, and Filipino English** accents, with expansion ongoing.

## Domain-Tuned Pronunciation

The differentiating feature is what Deepgram calls domain-specific terminology accuracy. Standard TTS models are trained on general web text — they've seen the word "propranolol" maybe once. Aura-2 is tuned to correctly pronounce medical terminology, financial instruments, and legal language without the hesitations or mispronunciations that make generic TTS models unusable in professional contexts.

The use cases that benefit most:
- **Healthcare:** Patient communication, clinical documentation readback, appointment reminders
- **Finance:** Portfolio updates, market briefings, earnings call summaries
- **Legal:** Document readback, contract summaries, compliance notifications

## Performance

Latency sits at **sub-200ms** — not Cartesia-fast, but well within the threshold for conversational applications. The pricing is **$0.030 per 1,000 characters**, which is competitive with Google and Azure.

On-premises deployment is available for organizations with data residency requirements.

## Context

Deepgram's STT background gives Aura-2 an interesting edge: the team understands how audio will ultimately be consumed because they've spent years building systems that process it. That signal-level understanding shows in output quality — Aura-2 consistently scores well in naturalness evaluations for spoken content that ends up being transcribed or analyzed downstream.

If your use case is healthcare, finance, or any domain where mispronounced terminology means credibility loss, Aura-2 is worth a serious evaluation before defaulting to the obvious choices.
