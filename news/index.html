TTS News

Model releases, provider updates, and research worth knowing about.

  • March 6, 2026 · research open-source voice-design instruction-following

    VoiceSculptor: Design a Voice With Words, Not Sliders

    Northwestern Polytechnical University releases VoiceSculptor — an open-source system combining instruction-following voice design (describe pitch, age, emotion, style in plain language) with high-fidelity voice cloning in a single framework.

  • March 6, 2026 · research open-source arabic multilingual

    Habibi: The First Unified TTS Model for 20+ Arabic Dialects

    Shanghai Jiao Tong University's X-LANCE Lab releases Habibi — an open-source TTS suite unifying 20+ Arabic dialects with zero-shot voice cloning, outperforming leading commercial services. From the author of F5-TTS.

  • March 6, 2026 · research open-source alibaba streaming

    CosyVoice 2: Alibaba's Streaming TTS Hits Human-Parity Naturalness

    CosyVoice 2 achieves human-parity naturalness in streaming mode by combining a pre-trained LLM backbone with chunk-aware causal flow matching — single model handles both streaming and non-streaming synthesis.

  • March 5, 2026 · release cartesia

    Cartesia Releases Sonic 3: Sub-80ms Latency, Real Laughter, and 40+ Languages

    Cartesia's third-generation Sonic model raises the bar for real-time voice agents with emotional expression, instant cloning, and enterprise-grade compliance.

  • March 4, 2026 · spotlight deepgram

    Deepgram Aura-2: 40+ Named English Voices Built for Domain-Specific Accuracy

    Deepgram's Aura-2 brings 40+ mythologically-named English voices with sub-200ms latency and domain-tuned pronunciation for healthcare, finance, and legal applications.

  • February 28, 2026 · open-source release sesame

    Sesame CSM-1B: The First TTS Model Built Specifically for Conversation

    Sesame's Conversational Speech Model is a 1B-parameter open-source model designed from the ground up for dialog — realistic turn-taking, natural back-channels, and Apache 2.0 licensing.

  • February 25, 2026 · open-source release canopy-labs

    Orpheus TTS: Canopy Labs Releases 3B Open-Source Model with 8 Distinct Voices

    Canopy Labs' Orpheus is a 3-billion-parameter Apache 2.0 TTS model delivering eight named voices with expressive, natural output that competes with top cloud providers.

  • February 20, 2026 · open-source research f5-tts

    F5-TTS: Flow-Matching Architecture Delivers Fast, High-Quality Open TTS

    F5-TTS uses a flow-matching architecture to achieve fast inference and high output quality under an MIT license — filling the gap between tiny edge models and large 3B+ heavyweights.

  • February 15, 2026 · release open-source kani efficiency

    Kani-TTS-2: 400M Parameters, 3GB VRAM, Voice Cloning — Trained in 6 Hours

    nineninesix.ai releases Kani-TTS-2 — a 400M parameter model using LiquidAI's LFM2 architecture and NVIDIA NanoCodec. Zero-shot voice cloning on 3GB VRAM, with a Real-Time Factor of 0.2. Apache 2.0.

  • February 2, 2026 · release elevenlabs

    ElevenLabs Eleven v3 Is Now Generally Available

    Eleven v3 exits alpha and goes GA — bringing audio tags, multi-speaker dialogue, and 70+ language support. The most expressive TTS model ElevenLabs has shipped.

  • January 22, 2026 · release open-source alibaba multilingual

    Qwen3-TTS: Alibaba Releases Open-Source TTS Trained on 5 Million Hours

    Alibaba Cloud's Qwen team releases Qwen3-TTS — two model sizes (0.6B and 1.7B), 10 languages, voice cloning, free-form voice design via natural language, and streaming support. Apache 2.0.

  • January 21, 2026 · release inworld pricing

    Inworld TTS-1.5 Claims Top Quality Benchmark at $10/1M Chars — 25x Cheaper Than Alternatives

    Inworld AI enters the TTS market with TTS-1.5 — claiming ELO 1,160 benchmark performance (highest rated) at $5–10 per million characters. If the numbers hold, it's the most significant pricing disruption the TTS market has seen.

  • January 5, 2026 · industry meta acquisition migration

    Play.ht Is Gone: What Meta's Acquisition Means for TTS Developers

    Play.ht was acquired by Meta in July 2025 and shut down permanently on December 31st. The API went dark first in July, the full platform followed at year-end — forcing thousands of developers to migrate.
